MANY WORLDS LARGE ENSEMBLE

JOURNEY HOME

The MANY WORLDS LARGE ENSEMBLE is an open source, open process initiative with the goal of documenting and making available the process of composing for, organizing, rehearsing the ensemble.
JOURNEY HOME is a collection of the first 10 compositions written for the four winds, five brass, four strings, and four-piece rhythm section group by Alexei Ozerov, following their journey in learning to compose for the medium of large ensemble and navigate post-graduation life. Each piece is reflective of a moment or emotion since starting this process, accompanying Alexei through the beginning days of the pandemic, their partner moving to Canada after 6 years of long-distance, getting married, taking a day job, and working through the ups and downs of the creative process and striving to do their best while following their goals and aspirations as a musician.
These events and that journey constitute the themes of the music Alexei has written - each reflecting a piece of the search to find a place for themselves as a composer and person in the past years. The difficult process of understanding how to express oneself in the art-form they desperately wants to be able to practice. Finding oneself and defining oneâ€™s relationship to creativity, creation, the process, and the failures and successes that come with it.
Each major event has been laid out below in the JOURNEY'S MAP, linking every step towards bringing this music to life with a snapshot of what circumstances, thoughts, challenges, and decisions were happening at the time. This covers both compositional process, hearing the music played for the first time, applying for grants, organizing and recording a demo, and striving to get the funding necessary for recording this music.
Thank you for going on this journey with me - I hope that you find something useful for yourself in it.
